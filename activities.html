Student Life at St. Ann

Get Involved

St. Ann Catholic School offers a variety of extracurricular activities that enrich student life beyond the classroom. From faith-based service to music, academic competition, and student leadership, there is something for every student to explore, grow, and shine. Activities are open to eligible students in Grades 3 through 8.

Faith & Service

Altar Servers
Grades 3–8

Students are trained to serve at Mass, assisting the priest and deacon during the liturgy. Altar service is a privilege that deepens students' understanding and love of the Catholic faith.

Activity leaderDeacon Gary
Mass Choir
Grades 4–8

Students are trained to lead the singing at school Mass, helping to lift the community in worship through song. A wonderful opportunity for students with a love of music and faith.

AdvisorMs. Attaway

Music & Arts

Band
Grades 4–8

Band meets weekly and gives students the opportunity to learn and develop instrumental music skills. Fees for band participation are paid directly to the band teacher.

Activity leaderMrs. Carter
Chorus
Grades 4–8

Chorus meets weekly during the school day. Members perform for school and community events throughout the year, sharing their talents with families and the wider Fayetteville community.

Activity leaderMrs. Carter

Academic & Leadership

Battle of the Books
Grades 4–8

Students who meet eligibility requirements join the team to prepare for the annual Battle of the Books competition — a reading-based academic challenge that promotes a love of literature.

CoachMrs. Coughlin
National Junior Beta Club
Grades 6–8

The National Junior Beta Club fosters the qualities of responsibility, leadership, and respect, allowing students to have a voice in school matters and initiate meaningful school projects.

Faculty advisorMrs. Desjardin
Spelling Bee
Grades 4–8

The annual Spelling Bee is held each year around December. Students compete to demonstrate their vocabulary and spelling skills in front of their school community.

CoordinatorTo be announcedHeld annually in December
