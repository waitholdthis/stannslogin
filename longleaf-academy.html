New Tool for St. Ann Students

St. Ann Catholic School is pleased to make Longleaf Academy available as a specialized academic support resource for our students. This program is designed to help students who learn differently reach their full academic potential through proven, research-based instruction.

Empowering Every Learner

Founded in 2009 and operating as an exclusive resource program since 2016, Longleaf Academy partners with area private schools — including St. Ann — to provide specialized instruction for students with dyslexia and language-based learning differences. Their mission: to empower children to become self-sufficient, intrinsic learners equipped to achieve academically.

What Is Longleaf Academy?

Longleaf Academy is a non-profit educational resource program that specializes in teaching students with dyslexia and language-based learning disabilities. Rather than operating as a standalone school, Longleaf functions as a collaborative partner with private schools — working alongside St. Ann to extend the quality of support available to students who need it most.

Their approach uses research-based curriculum that is multi-sensory, scientifically sound, systematic, and cumulative. Instruction engages visual, auditory, and kinesthetic-tactile pathways simultaneously — meeting students where they are and building lasting skills over time.

How It Works

Orton-Gillingham Approach

The gold standard for dyslexia instruction — a structured, multi-sensory method backed by over 70 years of research and used by top specialists worldwide.

Individualized Instruction

A 4:1 student-to-teacher ratio ensures every student receives targeted, personalized attention tailored to their specific learning profile.

Multi-Sensory Learning

Instruction simultaneously engages visual, auditory, and kinesthetic-tactile neurological pathways to reinforce learning at every level.

School Partnership Model

Longleaf collaborates directly with private schools like St. Ann to extend specialized support without requiring students to change schools.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is this program for?

Longleaf Academy is designed for students with dyslexia and language-based learning differences who need specialized, structured literacy and math instruction. If your child struggles with reading, writing, spelling, or math in ways that don't respond to traditional classroom approaches, Longleaf may be a strong fit.

Does my child have to leave St. Ann to use it?

No. Longleaf Academy operates as a collaborative resource partner with St. Ann. Students continue their enrollment here while accessing specialized instruction through Longleaf's program.

Is financial assistance available?

Yes. Longleaf Academy offers tuition assistance for eligible families. Contact Longleaf directly to learn more about available aid and the application process.

Is there a summer program?

Yes. Longleaf Academy offers a Summer Academy for students who want to continue building skills over the summer months. Contact Longleaf for current summer session dates and availability.

Who teaches the program?

Longleaf has five credentialed Orton-Gillingham teachers and coaches on staff, including founder Jill Dejak, a Fellow of Record in the Orton-Gillingham approach.

For the most current information on enrollment, scheduling, and tuition assistance, visit longleafacademy.org or contact Longleaf Academy directly.
