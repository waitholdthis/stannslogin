Mission & Vision

Our commitment to Catholic education excellence

Home / About / Mission & Vision

Our Mission

"Providing a quality education in a structured environment where academic excellence and Catholic identity are priorities is at the core of our school mission."

St. Ann Catholic School is a culturally diverse Catholic school that serves the entire community by promoting academic, spiritual, physical, and moral excellence in a safe, structured environment.

We are committed to educating the whole child, recognizing students' multiple intelligences and varied interests. Our safe, nurturing learning environment establishes strong bonds with all students and their families.

Our Vision

"Enter to Learn; Depart to Serve"

Our vision is to prepare students who enter to learn and depart to serve, equipped with the knowledge, character, and faith to make a positive difference in their communities and the world.

We envision graduates who are academically prepared, spiritually grounded, and morally committed to living Gospel values throughout their lives.

Our Core Values

Faith

Rooted in Catholic teaching and Gospel values, fostering spiritual growth and a personal relationship with God.

Excellence

Committed to academic rigor and high expectations while nurturing each student's unique gifts and talents.

Community

Building strong partnerships between school, parish, and families to support every child's growth.

Service

Teaching students to serve others with compassion and to be responsible stewards of God's gifts.

Inclusion

Welcoming students of all races, cultures, and backgrounds, reflecting the universal nature of God's love.

Integrity

Living with honesty, respect, and moral courage in all aspects of school life and beyond.

Our Commitment to Families

We are committed to partnering with families to provide an education that not only prepares students academically but also forms them in faith and character. Our licensed professional faculty maintains high expectations while providing the individual attention each student deserves.

As the first all-inclusive school in North Carolina, we are especially proud of our tradition of welcoming students from all backgrounds, including our strong support for military families who have been part of our community for over 68 years.

Quick Facts

  • Founded in 1956
  • 121 students enrolled
  • 11:1 student-teacher ratio
  • Cognia accredited
  • 50% military families
  • Diocese of Raleigh
