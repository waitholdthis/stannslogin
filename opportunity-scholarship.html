NC Opportunity Scholarships

The Opportunity Scholarship Program is a North Carolina state-funded initiative that provides working-class families with tuition assistance to attend the private school of their choice. Scholarships now range from approximately $3,000 to $7,000 annually, and eligibility requirements were recently expanded — more families qualify than ever before.

Applications for the 2026–2027 school year open February 2, 2026. Families may only officially apply through the NC State Education Assistance Authority website.

How to Apply

1

Check Your Eligibility

Visit the North Carolina Education Authority website to see if you may qualify based on income. This is a pre-eligibility determination only — it is not the official application.

ncseaa.edu/k12/opportunity/
2

Create a MyPortal Account

Set up your student portal account at the NC State Education Assistance Authority. You will use this account to submit your application and manage your scholarship.

myportal.ncseaa.edu
3

Submit an Application for Each Child

Beginning February 2, 2026, submit a separate application for each child you wish to receive a scholarship. You will receive a confirmation email from the NC State Education Assistance Authority for each application submitted.

One application per child — do not combine
4

Apply During the Priority Window

Applications received during the priority window are entered into a lottery and awarded scholarships. Applications submitted after the priority window are placed on a waiting list. Apply early for the best chance.

Priority window applications receive lottery placement
5

Enroll at St. Ann Catholic School

St. Ann Catholic School participates in the NC Opportunity Scholarship Program. Once your scholarship is awarded, contact our office to begin the enrollment process. Note that families must be prepared to pay the registration fee at the time of enrollment.

(910) 483-3216

Frequently Asked Questions

Are you eligible?

The income cap for the program was recently raised, meaning more families now qualify than in previous years. Visit the NC Education Authority website to check your pre-eligibility. This is not an official application — it simply lets you know whether to proceed with a full application.

How does it work?

Applications are accepted during a priority period each year. Those received within the priority window are selected through a lottery and awarded a scholarship. Applications received after the priority window are placed on a waiting list and may be awarded if additional funding becomes available.

How do I apply?

Create a MyPortal account at the NC State Education Assistance Authority to begin the application. You must submit a separate application for each child — do not combine children on one application. A confirmation email will be sent for each application submitted. Official applications open February 2, 2026.

Where can a scholarship be used?

Use NC Schools Around Me to find participating private schools near you, compare average tuition, and learn more. Schools continue to register for the program — always confirm directly with the school whether they accept the Opportunity Scholarship. St. Ann Catholic School participates in this program.

Already Have a Scholarship?

Renew for the 2026–2027 School Year

If you received an Opportunity Scholarship for the 2025–2026 school year, you must officially renew through the NC State Education Assistance Authority. Sign into MyPortal where you will find a renewal link for 2026–2027. If you also have children who did not use a scholarship last year, create a new application for each of them separately.

Apply for the Scholarship

Official applications open February 2, 2026. Apply through the NC State Education Assistance Authority.
