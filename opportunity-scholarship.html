North Carolina Opportunity Scholarship Program

The Diocese of Raleigh Catholic Schools financial assistance page describes the Opportunity Scholarship Program as a North Carolina school-choice program for Kindergarten through 12th grade students choosing a participating non-public school. The Diocese page lists funding of up to $7,686 per year for eligible children.

The Diocese source page lists the new student Opportunity Scholarship application opening on February 6, 2025, with the priority window closing on March 6, 2025. Families should verify current dates with NCSEAA.

Additional Scholarship & Aid Options

African American and Native American Ancestry Tuition Assistance

The Diocese of Raleigh Office of Education administers tuition assistance for Catholic school students in Preschool through 12th grade who are of African Ancestry or Native American heritage in the Diocese of Raleigh. The Diocese page states that awards are funded by parish second collection donations and a grant from the Black and Indian Mission Office.

Application Notes for Ancestry Tuition Assistance

The Diocese page states that applicants must complete a Catholic school enrollment application before the tuition assistance application is considered. It lists an application window opening on February 1, 2025 and closing on March 15, 2025, with decision letters emailed no later than May 24, 2025. Families should confirm current-year dates with the Diocese.

School-Based Financial Support

The Diocese notes that Catholic schools work to keep Catholic education affordable and partner with other entities that help families afford tuition. Families seeking school-based financial support are directed to create an account through FACTS Tuition Management.

How to Apply

1

Check Your Eligibility

Visit the North Carolina Education Authority website to see if you may qualify based on income. This is a pre-eligibility determination only — it is not the official application.

ncseaa.edu/k12/opportunity/
2

Create a MyPortal Account

Set up your student portal account at the NC State Education Assistance Authority. You will use this account to submit your application and manage your scholarship.

myportal.ncseaa.edu
3

Submit an Application for Each Child

Submit a separate application for each child you wish to receive a scholarship. You will receive a confirmation email from the NC State Education Assistance Authority for each application submitted.

One application per child — do not combine
4

Apply During the Priority Window

Applications received during the priority window are entered into a lottery and awarded scholarships. Applications submitted after the priority window are placed on a waiting list. Apply early for the best chance.

Priority window applications receive lottery placement
5

Enroll at St. Ann Catholic School

St. Ann Catholic School participates in the NC Opportunity Scholarship Program. Once your scholarship is awarded, contact our office to begin the enrollment process. Note that families must be prepared to pay the registration fee at the time of enrollment.

(910) 483-3216

Frequently Asked Questions

Are you eligible?

The income cap for the program was recently raised, meaning more families now qualify than in previous years. Visit the NC Education Authority website to check your pre-eligibility. This is not an official application — it simply lets you know whether to proceed with a full application. Families should verify the latest eligibility rules directly with NCSEAA.

How does it work?

Applications are accepted during a priority period each year. Those received within the priority window are selected through a lottery and awarded a scholarship. Applications received after the priority window are placed on a waiting list and may be awarded if additional funding becomes available.

How do I apply?

Create a MyPortal account at the NC State Education Assistance Authority to begin the application. You must submit a separate application for each child — do not combine children on one application. A confirmation email will be sent for each application submitted. Verify the current application window directly with NCSEAA.

Where can a scholarship be used?

Use NC Schools Around Me to find participating private schools near you, compare average tuition, and learn more. Schools continue to register for the program — always confirm directly with the school whether they accept the Opportunity Scholarship. St. Ann Catholic School participates in this program.

Already Have a Scholarship?

Renew Through NCSEAA MyPortal

If you already receive an Opportunity Scholarship, you must officially renew through the NC State Education Assistance Authority. Sign into MyPortal where you will find renewal information for the next school year. If you also have children who did not use a scholarship last year, create a new application for each of them separately.

Apply for Scholarships

Apply for the NC Opportunity Scholarship through NCSEAA. For school-based aid, use FACTS Tuition Management.
