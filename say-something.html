Sandy Hook Promise Program

If You See Something, Say Something

Anonymous Reporting — Available 24/7

If you see warning signs that someone may be in danger of hurting themselves or others, report it immediately. Your tip is anonymous and could save a life. Crisis counselors are available around the clock.

Call 1-844-5-SayNow Submit a Tip Online

If there is an immediate threat to life, call 911 first. The Say Something line is for non-emergency concerns where you want to report a warning sign anonymously.

About the Program

The Say Something program by Sandy Hook Promise teaches students in Kindergarten through 12th Grade to recognize the warning signs of someone who may be at risk of hurting themselves or others — and to speak up to a trusted adult before a tragedy occurs. Research has shown that nearly 100% of school attackers displayed warning signs before their attack, most often on social media or to people around them. Knowing the signs and having the courage to act can prevent violence before it happens.

Warning Signs to Watch For

Warning signs can appear in thoughts, feelings, actions, and behaviors — including online activity. A single sign doesn't always mean danger, but if you notice any of these, report it to a trusted adult right away.

Social Withdrawal

Suddenly pulling away from friends, family, and activities — including online or on social media.

Excessive Irritability

Becoming angry very quickly, showing little patience, or having extreme mood changes.

Bullying Others

Targeting others — especially based on race, religion, gender, or sexual orientation.

Chronic Loneliness or Isolation

Experiencing deep and lasting feelings of loneliness or being cut off from others.

Thoughts of Harm

Expressing persistent thoughts of hurting themselves or someone else.

Direct Threats

Making direct threats — verbal, written, or online — toward a person, place, or themselves.

Bragging About Weapons

Boasting about access to guns or other weapons, or showing them off.

Recruiting Others

Trying to recruit accomplices or an audience for a planned attack.

Obsession with Violence

Fixation on violent online content, mass shooters, or weapons — especially online.

Cruelty to Animals

Harming or showing cruelty toward animals, which can signal deeper behavioral concerns.

What You Can Report

You don't have to be certain something is wrong to report it. When in doubt, say something. The Say Something system handles all of the following concerns:

Violence (with or without weapons) Verbal or physical abuse Sexual abuse or harassment Social media threats Bullying & intimidation Weapon access or attack planning Depression, anxiety, or isolation Substance abuse Suicide threats or self-harm Any other troubling behavior

How to Submit a Tip

1

Choose How to Report

Call 1-844-5-SayNow (1-844-572-9669), submit a tip online at sandyhookpromise.org, or download the free Say Something app from the App Store or Google Play.

2

Share What You Know

Describe the warning signs or behavior you've observed. You can remain completely anonymous — you never have to share your name.

3

Chat with a Crisis Counselor

A trained crisis counselor will review your tip and follow up with you privately through a secure anonymous chat to gather any additional details.

4

The Right People Are Notified

If the concern is serious, the crisis counselor refers the information to school officials and/or law enforcement so the right help can reach the person at risk.
